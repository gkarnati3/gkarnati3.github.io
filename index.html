My Projects

SpectroSam

We assist in the speech-language education of children with hearing disabilities specifically ages 3-6 to help mainstream them into school as soon as possible. We are currently developing a method to deliver affordable supplemental Speech-Language Pathology therapy and practice through a mobile educationally entertaining game application that provides active and visual feedback. Check out our website here.


Sorting Hat

The interactive sorting hat imitates the Harry Potter lifestyle. Get a first hand experience of what house you would have been placed in after finally receiving that letter to Hogwarts you’ve always been waiting for. The sorting hat intelligently sorts you into different Hogwarts houses based on your yes or no responses to a series of questions. You can watch a video of it here.


Kinesse

We developed a pair of smart shorts that allows users to exercise more naturally We were funded through Georgia Tech's Create-X program, Idea-to-Prototype. We created a one-year plan for the prototyping by interviewing more than 60 consumers, identifying key problems for runners, looking into market trends, researching how to design for usability and aesthetic purposes, etc.


OvenBot

The advent of digital appliances has made life harder for those with vision impairments. OvenBot is an app that can help visually impaired users interact more efficiently with modern home appliances. This app maps out and vocalizes where the buttons are on the display and what they say based on the location of the users finger, which can improve user navigation around digital appliances. OvenBot was built as part of the Perkins Hackathon where we were the challenge winner. Here's a demo.


Synesthesia

Synesthesia is an application that allows users to "conduct" a song by changing pitch and color, dance to the song, as well as paint an abstract visual representation of their movements. We built the application on Unity and interfaced the Myo armbands in order to obtain user arm movement. Synesthesia was built at HackGT 2017. Here's a demo.


Tesla Coil

Designed a tesla coil from scratch at the Georgia Governor's Honors Program as a Physics Major during the 4 week intensive summer course. Here's a demo.


Housing Predictions

We used time (RNNs and LSTMs) and non-time (regression and other traditional ML models) based deep learning models to forecast local housing market trends. Read our paper on our findings and learn more about our implementation on github.


Bobot

We made an automated Boba machine that acts as a standalone salesman because it used Computer Vision to attract people towards it. Bobot has a easy to use UI that works like a vending machine. This allows people to make their own boba drink quick and fast. We made Bobot at MakeMIT and were one of the top 7 finalists. Here's a quick video.


Skin Cancer Analysis

Detecting skin cancer lesions is vital to patient outcomes, with a 5-year survival rate dropping from 99% to 14% depending on the stage of detection. We applied unsupervised, supervised, and transfer learning methods to images from the “Skin Lesion Analysis Toward Melanoma Detection 2018" dataset to find the best accuracies. See our presentation results here and our implementation on our github.


Mr. Tutor Finances

My sister and I are creating an accounts management database for my parents who own a small business for tutoring. This helps them keep up with their students, attendance, fees, etc. We also created a parent portal side to give all their students' parents the ability to keep up with their own accounts.


Mr. Tutor Questionnaire

My sister and I are creating a Questionnaire software platform that allows our parents' small business manage all their practice questions, create quizzes, assign them to students, manage students in different classes, and track their students' progress. This platform allows tutors to expand iterative and differentiated practice to their students.


Google Home Stories

I was exploring my google home apps one day when I stumbled on american bed time stories for kids. I had been trying to refine my ability to read telugu (my mother tongue) over quarantine so after seeing these stories I thought it could be fun to make a similar one but for telugu short stories for kids while refining my own reading skills. As I started to develop the google home action and started recording my mom reading these stories, we thought it would be nice to add a translation in as well. Skippable but still available. Publishing soon for you to try out yourself from home so keep a look out!
