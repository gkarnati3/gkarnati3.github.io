My Projects

SpectroSam

We assist in the speech-language education of children with hearing disabilities specifically ages 3-6 to help mainstream them into school as soon as possible. We are currently developing a method to deliver affordable supplemental Speech-Language Pathology therapy and practice through a mobile educationally entertaining game application that provides active and visual feedback.


Sorting Hat

The interactive sorting hat imitates the Harry Potter lifestyle. Get a first hand experience of what house you would have been placed in after finally receiving that letter to Hogwarts you’ve always been waiting for. The sorting hat intelligently sorts you into different Hogwarts houses based on your yes or no responses to a series of questions. You can watch a video of it here.


Kinesse

We're looking to develop a pair of smart shorts that allows users to exercise more naturally We were funded through Georgia Tech's Create-X program, Idea-to-Prototype. We created a one-year plan for the prototyping by interviewing more than 60 consumers, identifying key problems for runners, looking into market trends, researching how to design for usability and aesthetic purposes, etc.


OvenBot

The advent of digital appliances has made life harder for those with vision impairments. OvenBot is an app that can help visually impaired users interact more efficiently with modern home appliances. This app maps out and vocalizes where the buttons are on the display and what they say based on the location of the users finger, which can improve user navigation around digital appliances. OvenBot was built as part of the Perkins Hackathon. Here's a demo.


Synesthesia

Synesthesia is an application that allows users to "conduct" a song by changing pitch and color, dance to the song, as well as paint an abstract visual representation of their movements. We built the application on Unity and interfaced the Myo armbands in order to obtain user arm movement. Here's a demo.


Tesla Coil

Designed a tesla coil from scratch at the Georgia Governor's Honors Program as a Physics Major during the 4 week intensive summer course. Here's a demo.

Bobot

We made an automated Boba machine that acts as a standalone salesman because it used Computer Vision to attract people towards it. Bobot has a easy to use UI that works like a vending machine. This allows people to make their own boba drink quick and fast. We made Bobot at MakeMIT and were one of the top 7 finalists. Here's a quick video.


Skin Cancer Analysis

Detecting skin cancer lesions is vital to patient outcomes, with a 5-year survival rate dropping from 99% to 14% depending on the stage of detection. We applied unsupervised, supervised, and transfer learning methods to images from the “Skin Lesion Analysis Toward Melanoma Detection 2018" dataset to find the best accuracies.
